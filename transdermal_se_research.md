# Transdermal HRT Side Effects Research

## Most Common Side Effects (by frequency from Drugs.com 180 reviews):
- Itching: 18.9%
- Hot flashes: 16.7%
- Rash: 7.8%
- Redness: 6.1%
- Headaches: 5.6%
- Insomnia: 3.9%
- Night sweats: 3.9%
- Sweating: 2.8%
- Burning: 2.2%
- Depression: 2.2%

## Skin-specific reactions (most relevant for our use case):
1. Redness/irritation at application site
2. Itching under/around the patch
3. Rash or hives at application site
4. Burning sensation
5. Blistering (rare but reported)
6. Skin breakdown/scarring

## Real patient language patterns:
- "it starts to itch and turn red"
- "redness, severe itching, and burning"
- "skin was completely red and rashed out"
- "itch/rash and associated redness"
- "itches like crazy. My skin is red"
- "open blisters and redness that are painful and itchy"
- "skin irritation and redness at application site"
- "horrible red patches that hurt and itch"

## Key observations for realistic text:
- Patients use simple, casual language
- They mention specific symptoms (redness, itching, burning)
- They often mention location ("where I put the patch", "at the application site")
- They describe duration ("takes weeks to heal", "marks for months")
- They're brief and matter-of-fact
- Common pattern: 1-2 symptoms + location + brief impact
